What is SIFT?

SIFT is a four-step strategy used to fact-check and cross-reference information. It can be applied to quickly ascertain the accuracy of information presented in social media and news articles.

The steps are outlined below:

S

STOP

and think before sharing a post or article

I

INVESTIGATE

the source. Check their reputation and authority.

F

FIND

better coverage. Can you find similar claims elsewhere?

T

TRACE

claims, quotes, and media to its original context.

How do we extract data from the articles?

The URL provided goes through a Python script that scrapes information from the article link. This is done through third party libraries like Newspaper and Beautiful Soup This is means that most of the information extracted is simply finding the right elements that already exist in the link you provided like the date published, authors, titles etc. However, certain elements may not be entirely accurate like summary and keywords extracted from the article depends entirely on third party natural language processing power.

Target Stakeholders and Users

Anyone who uses facebook and messenger are our users but our target stakeholders would be those in the age range of 25-55 who frequently use Facebook as their news outlet.

Our Actions

We wanted to produce a product that would help individuals identify credible sources. Our goal is not to tell users what sources are credible and what aren’t, but rather our aim is to be an information hub where we provide the tools and skill sets for users to decide for themselves whether or not a source is credible.

What Our Product Provides

Our product provides efficiency and efficacy as we aim to draw out key details in a source that can help a user determine if a source is credible or not. Our aim is to effectively help users fact check while also saving them time.
